## It's 4pm. You've been busy all day. Nothing moved.

You know the feeling.

You started the morning with a list. Then a Slack thread pulled you sideways. Then a rep needed something. Then a pipeline call ran long. Then you spent 45 minutes writing an email you'd been avoiding. Then someone asked for a report you had to build from scratch.

Now it's 4pm and the list looks exactly the same as it did at 8am, except you're tired.

This isn't a time management problem. It's a leverage problem.

Most front-line managers — especially BDR and SDR managers in their first or second role — are quietly doing the work of three or four people. You're the strategist, the enablement team, the writer, the analyst, and the coach. All at once. Usually with no admin support, a lean budget, and a team that needs you present.

Nobody tells you that when they hand you the title.

## What this is

This is a collection of 8 Claude prompts I've built and refined while managing a BDR team in healthcare tech.

Each one handles a specific task that used to eat 30–90 minutes of my day. Not by doing the thinking for me — but by doing the setup work, the first draft, the framework, so I can spend my time on the part that actually requires a human: judgment, relationship, presence.

These aren't magic. They're tools. And like any tool, they work better when you understand what they're for.

Every skill in this playbook includes:
- **What it's for** — the specific moment it solves
- **The base prompt** — copy, paste, go
- **The customization prompt** — one step to make it yours, tailored to your team, your industry, your voice

You don't need to be technical. You don't need a Claude subscription beyond the free tier for most of these. You just need to know what you're trying to get done.

## Before you start

Three things that will make every prompt in here work better:

**1. Give context, not just tasks.**
The difference between a mediocre output and a useful one is almost always the context you give upfront. Don't just say "write me a sequence." Tell Claude who the buyer is, what they care about, what usually stops the deal, and what your team's tone sounds like. The prompts in this guide walk you through that.

**2. Treat the first output as a draft, not a final.**
Claude is fast. That doesn't mean the first thing it produces is the thing you use. Read it. Edit it. Put your voice in it. The goal is to start at 70% instead of 0% — and get to 90% faster than you would have alone.

**3. Make it yours.**
Every skill in this playbook has a customization prompt at the bottom. Run it once, save your output, and paste it into future prompts as context. That's how you go from generic outputs to something that actually sounds like it came from your team.

## Make it yours — master customization prompt

Run this once in Claude before you start. Save the output. Reference it in any future prompt.

```
I want you to help me customize a series of management prompts to fit my specific context.

Ask me the following questions one at a time and wait for my answer before moving on:

1. What is your role and title?
2. What industry or vertical are you selling into?
3. Who are the 2-3 buyer personas your team targets most?
4. What does your team use for sequencing / outreach? (e.g. Outreach.io, Salesloft, HubSpot)
5. What does your team use for calls? (e.g. Nooks, Gong, Orum)
6. How would you describe your coaching style in 2-3 sentences?
7. What's the one thing your team struggles with most right now?

After I answer all 7, give me a single "context block" I can paste into any future prompt to personalize the output automatically.
```

That's what this playbook is actually trying to give back. Not just hours. Presence.
