# Skill 02 — The Talk Track Generator

> *"A talk track isn't a script. It's a thinking frame. The rep who sounds most natural on a cold call is the one who knows the structure so well they don't have to think about it."*

---

## What this is for

Your team is heading into Nooks. Or a cold call block. Or a new campaign where the persona is unfamiliar and the messaging isn't locked in yet.

A talk track gives them the structure to sound confident without sounding rehearsed. The opening, the pivot, the discovery questions, the handle for the three most common objections, and a clean close. All built for one specific persona, one specific pain, one specific ask.

This prompt builds that in under five minutes. Not a generic cold call framework — a talk track calibrated to your product, your buyer, and the opening move that actually works for your team.

---

## The moment this solves

A rep is three to four weeks in. They've done the product training, they've shadowed some calls, they've sent their first emails. Now it's time to actually dial and everything they learned in onboarding evaporates the moment someone picks up.

Or your experienced rep is breaking into a new persona and keeps getting stuck after the opener. They know the product cold. They don't know this buyer's world. The talk track closes that gap — not by giving them a script to read, but by giving them a structure they can internalize before the call block starts.

---

## The base prompt

```
Build me a cold call talk track for my BDR team. This is for a cold outbound 
call — no prior contact, no warm intro.

Here is the context:

**Company and product:**
[What you sell and the one-line value prop that actually lands.]

**Target persona:**
[Title, company type, and the pain they feel most acutely.]

**Opening move:**
Permission ask — open by asking for 30 seconds before pitching anything.

**The 30-second value statement:**
[What does the rep say if they get the yes? Pain-led, not a product pitch.]

**Three most common objections:**
[What does your team hear most?]

**The ask:**
[What is the rep asking for at the end of a good call?]

**Tone:**
[How should this sound?]

---

Build the talk track in this format:

**THE OPENER**
[The permission ask — word for word.]

**IF YES — THE 30 SECONDS**
[The value statement. Pain-first. No feature lists.]

**THE PIVOT QUESTION**
[The one question that opens the real conversation.]

**DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (2-3)**
[Questions that help the rep understand fit and urgency.]

**OBJECTION HANDLES**
For each of the three objections:
- Restate / Reframe / Re-ask

**THE CLOSE**
[Two versions: if going well / if lukewarm]

**THE VOICEMAIL**
[20 seconds, references the email by subject line if there's a parallel sequence.]

---

After the full talk track, give me a coaching note — 2-3 sentences on what 
separates the reps who use this well from the reps who don't.
```

## The Nooks prep card variant

When your team needs a quick reference card for a call block:

```
Based on the talk track above, give me a one-page Nooks prep card my reps 
can have open during a call block.

Format:
PERSONA:
PAIN:
OPENER:
30 SECONDS:
PIVOT QUESTION:
TOP 3 OBJECTIONS + ONE-LINE HANDLES:
THE ASK:
VOICEMAIL:

No prose. No headers beyond the labels. Should fit on one screen.
```

---

## The objection-only prompt

When your team knows the track but keeps getting stuck on one specific objection:

```
My BDR team sells [product] to [persona]. We keep running into this objection:

"[Exact objection wording]"

Build me three different ways to handle this objection:
1. The empathy-first handle
2. The curiosity handle — respond with a question
3. The direct handle — name what's really happening

For each: exact words, when to use it, and the re-ask that follows.
Then give me a coaching note: the mindset shift, not just the words.
```

---

## Make it yours

```
I want to build a talk track context block. Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. What's the opening move that works best for your team right now?
2. What's the one thing prospects almost always say in the first 30 seconds?
3. How do your best reps sound different from your average reps on a cold call?
4. What's the objection your team hears most, and what's the handle that's been working?
5. What does a great cold call outcome look like for your team?

After I answer, write me a call context block.
```

## Tips from the field

**The permission ask only works if you mean it.**
If your rep asks for 30 seconds and talks for two minutes regardless, the technique backfires. Train your reps on what to do when someone says no.

**The pivot question is the most valuable part.**
Ask the question. Stop. Count to five. Let them fill the silence.

**"Don't thank them for saying yes."**
Go straight into the value statement. The best compliment you can pay someone's time is using it well, not thanking them for it.

**The voicemail should reference the email.**
When call and email are clearly coordinated, it signals a real campaign. Makes the email more likely to get opened.
