# Skill 07 — The Play Launcher

> *"Not every signal is worth a full play. The first decision isn't what to say — it's whether to say anything at all."*

---

## What this is for

Your team lives for a good signal. A prospect publishes a piece about a major initiative. A health system announces they're sending a team to HIMSS. A CNO posts about nurse retention being the focus of their next fiscal year.

These moments are real openings. The problem is knowing what size response they deserve — and then moving fast enough to matter before the moment cools.

A full play is three channels, coordinated messaging, a brief for the whole team, and a follow-up structure. A single personalized touch is one email that references what they said and asks one question.

Most teams default to one or the other regardless of the signal. This prompt helps you make the right call first — then builds whatever the signal actually warrants.

---

## The moment this solves

You're scanning LinkedIn and a CNO at a target account just published a thought leadership piece about improving nurse communication workflows. That's your space. That's your buyer. That's a real opening.

You have two problems: deciding how big to go, and going fast enough that the signal is still warm.

---

## The triage prompt

Run this first, before you build anything:

```
Help me decide how to respond to a sales signal before I build a play.

The signal:
[Describe exactly what happened. Quote the headline, the conference name, 
the initiative they announced. Be specific.]

The account context:
[What do you know about this account? Prior contact? Key stakeholders?]

Our product fit:
[One sentence on why this signal matters for your solution.]

---

Tell me:

1. Signal strength: High / Medium / Low — and the one-sentence reason.

2. Response type:
   - Full play (3+ touches, multi-channel, team brief)
   - Targeted sequence (3-5 touch email + call, single rep)
   - Single personalized touch

3. The window: How long does this signal stay warm?

4. The one thing to reference: What specific detail proves this is real 
   outreach, not spray-and-pray?

5. Risk of over-reaching: Any reason to be careful here?
```

## The full play prompt

```
Build a signal-based outbound play for my BDR team.

**The signal:** [What happened and when. Be specific.]
**The account:** [ICP fit, prior contact, key stakeholders.]
**Why this matters for us:** [One sentence.]
**Team context:** [Who is running this? How many accounts in the cluster?]
**Channels:** Email-first, then call, then LinkedIn.
**Timeline:** [Days for the play to run.]

---

Build:

PLAY NAME: [Short, descriptive, something the team can refer to it by]

WHY NOW: [One paragraph for the team brief. Read aloud in 90 seconds.]

THE MESSAGING SPINE: [The relevance frame in one sentence. Not the pitch.]

TOUCH SEQUENCE:
Touch 1 — Day 1 — Email
- Subject line
- Body (short, references signal in first sentence, ends with one question)
- Personalization note

Touch 2 — Day 3 — Phone
- Opening line (references email and signal)
- 30-second value statement
- Voicemail (20 seconds, names the article/conference specifically)

Touch 3 — Day 5 — LinkedIn
- Connection note or content comment (one sentence, genuine)

Touch 4 — Day 8 — Email
- Different angle from Touch 1, shorter, ends with ask

Touch 5 — Day 12 — Close
- Call opening + close email

THE QUALIFICATION QUESTION: [Tells you if this is real or a dead end.]

SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE: [What counts as a win before a meeting is booked?]
```

## The conference play

For HIMSS, HLTH, Becker's — or any event your target is attending:

```
Build a conference-based outbound play.

**Account and persona:** [Who, title, what we know]
**Conference:** [Name, dates, their involvement — attending, presenting, sponsoring?]
**Our presence:** [Are we there? Booth? Customers presenting?]
**Timeline:** [How far out are we starting?]

Build three phases:

PHASE 1 — PRE-CONFERENCE (4-5 days before)
2 touches. Goal: get on their radar before they build their agenda.

PHASE 2 — DURING (if team is present)
1-2 touches or in-person play. Goal: face-to-face or a reply that opens a post-conference call.

PHASE 3 — POST-CONFERENCE (within 72 hours of close)
2 touches. Reference something specific — a session, a topic trending at the event.

For each phase: messaging angle, channel, and the specific signal reference.
```

## The single touch prompt

When triage says one email is right:

```
Write one personalized email based on a signal. Just one touch.

**The signal:** [What happened]
**The prospect:** [Name, title, company]
**Why we're relevant:** [One sentence]
**The ask:** [Small — a question, not a meeting invite]

Under 100 words. Opens with the specific signal (not "I saw your post" — 
something that shows you actually read it). Ends with one low-friction question.

Then give me one alternative with a completely different angle.
```

## Make it yours

```
Build me a signal triage framework for my team. Ask me:

1. What signal types have actually converted to pipeline in the last 6 months?
2. What's a signal that felt exciting but went nowhere — what did you learn?
3. What accounts or personas are worth a full play regardless of signal strength?
4. How fast does your team need to move when a signal hits?
5. What do your reps consistently get wrong when they respond to a signal solo?

Then build me a one-page signal triage guide — a decision tree that tells 
them what response to run based on signal, account, and timing.
```

## Tips from the field

**The triage decision is the skill.** Build it into your team's muscle memory. Running the triage question in standup — "here's a signal, what do we do with it?" — builds judgment faster than any training.

**Reference the signal in the first sentence. Not the second.** Front-loading the signal tells them immediately this isn't a template.

**The conference window closes in days.** Pre-conference outreach 4-5 days out hits the sweet spot. Post-conference follow-up within 72 hours converts dramatically higher than the same email a week later.

**"Success looks like" changes how your team executes.** Define intermediate wins so reps don't push too hard and burn the signal.

**Don't make it feel like surveillance.** Referencing a LinkedIn post they liked feels creepy. Referencing an article they wrote feels human. The difference is depth of engagement.
